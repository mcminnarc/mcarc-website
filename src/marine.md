# Marine Radio Information

Information about marine VHF radio for boaters in the McMinn County area.

## About Marine VHF

Marine VHF radio operates on 156-162 MHz and is the primary communication method for boaters. Unlike ham radio, no license exam is required for recreational boaters in US waters.

## Common Marine Channels

| Channel | Frequency | Use |
|---------|-----------|-----|
| 16 | 156.800 MHz | **Distress, Safety & Calling** |
| 9 | 156.450 MHz | Alternate calling (recreational) |
| 13 | 156.650 MHz | Bridge-to-bridge navigation |
| 22A | 157.100 MHz | Coast Guard liaison |
| 68-72 | Various | Non-commercial working |

<div class="info-box important">
<strong>Channel 16 (156.800 MHz)</strong> is the international distress and calling frequency. Monitor it at all times when your radio is on. Use it only for distress calls and initial contact, then move to a working channel.
</div>

## Radio Types

| Type | Power | Best For |
|------|-------|----------|
| Fixed Mount | 25W | Boats with electrical systems |
| Handheld | 5-6W | Small boats, kayaks, emergencies |

Look for radios with:
- DSC (Digital Selective Calling)
- Weather alert
- GPS integration

## MMSI Number

If your radio has DSC capability, register for a free MMSI number:
- [BoatUS](https://www.boatus.com/mmsi)
- [Sea Tow](https://www.seatow.com/mmsi)

This allows automatic position transmission during distress calls.
